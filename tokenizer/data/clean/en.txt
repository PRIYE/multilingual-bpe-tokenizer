**India**, officially the **Republic of India**,[[j]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-28)[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-29) is a country in [South Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asia "South Asia").  It is the [seventh-largest country by area](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_and_dependencies_by_area "List of countries and dependencies by area"), the [most populous country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_and_dependencies_by_population "List of countries and dependencies by population") in the world[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-30) and, since its independence in 1947, the world's most populous democracy.[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-31)[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-stein-arnold-32)[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFisher2018184–185-33) Bounded by the [Indian Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Ocean "Indian Ocean") on the south, the [Arabian Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabian_Sea "Arabian Sea") on the southwest, and the [Bay of Bengal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal "Bay of Bengal") on the southeast, it shares land borders with [Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakistan "Pakistan") to the west;[[k]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-35) [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China"), [Nepal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nepal "Nepal") and [Bhutan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bhutan "Bhutan") to the north; [Bangladesh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangladesh "Bangladesh") and [Myanmar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myanmar "Myanmar") to the east. In the Indian Ocean, India is near [Sri Lanka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sri_Lanka "Sri Lanka") and the [Maldives](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maldives "Maldives"). Its [Andaman and Nicobar Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andaman_and_Nicobar_Islands "Andaman and Nicobar Islands") share a [maritime border](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maritime_boundary "Maritime boundary") with Myanmar, [Thailand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thailand "Thailand") and [Indonesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indonesia "Indonesia").

[Modern humans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Modern_humans "Modern humans") arrived on the [Indian subcontinent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_subcontinent "Indian subcontinent") from [Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa "Africa") no later than 55,000 years ago.[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-PetragliaAllchin-36)[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Dyson2018p1-37)[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Fisher2018p23-38) Their long occupation, predominantly in isolation as hunter-gatherers, has made the region highly diverse.[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Dyson2018-28a-39) [Settled life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neolithic "Neolithic") emerged on the subcontinent in the western margins of the [Indus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indus_River "Indus River") [river basin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/River_basin "River basin") 9,000 years ago, evolving gradually into the [Indus Valley Civilisation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indus_Valley_Civilisation "Indus Valley Civilisation") of the third millennium BCE.[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Combined-2-40) By 1200 BCE, an [archaic form](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-language "Proto-language") of [Sanskrit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sanskrit "Sanskrit"), an [Indo-European language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indo-European_language "Indo-European language"), had [diffused](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trans-cultural_diffusion "Trans-cultural diffusion") into India from the northwest.[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-lowe1-41)[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Combined-4-Rigveda-42) [Its hymns](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rig_Veda "Rig Veda") recorded the [early dawnings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Historical_Vedic_religion "Historical Vedic religion") of [Hinduism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hinduism "Hinduism") in India.[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Combined-3-43) India's pre-existing [Dravidian languages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dravidian_languages "Dravidian languages") were supplanted in the northern regions.[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Combined-4-44) By 400 BCE, [caste](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caste "Caste") had emerged within Hinduism,[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Dyson2018-16a-45) and [Buddhism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_Buddhism_in_India "History of Buddhism in India") and [Jainism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_Jainism "History of Jainism") had arisen, proclaiming [social orders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_order "Social order") unlinked to heredity.[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Fisher2018-59-46) Early political consolidations gave rise to the loose-knit [Maurya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maurya_Empire "Maurya Empire") and [Gupta Empires](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gupta_Empire "Gupta Empire").[[36]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Combined-5-47) During this era, there was a flourishing of creativity in art, architecture, and writing,[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Combined-6-48) the status of women declined,[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Combined-7-49) and [untouchability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Untouchability "Untouchability") became an organised belief.[[l]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-51)[[39]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKulkeRothermund2016122–123-50) In [South India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_India "South India"), the [Middle kingdoms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_kingdoms_of_India#The_Deccan_plateau_and_South "Middle kingdoms of India") exported Dravidian language scripts and religious cultures to the kingdoms of [Southeast Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southeast_Asia "Southeast Asia").[[40]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-AsherAsher2006-17-52)

In the [1st millennium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1st_millennium "1st millennium") [Islam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam "Islam"), [Christianity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christianity "Christianity"), [Judaism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Judaism "Judaism"), and [Zoroastrianism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zoroastrianism "Zoroastrianism") became established on India's southern and western coasts.[[41]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Combined-8-53) Early in the [2nd millennium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2nd_millennium "2nd millennium") Muslim armies from [Central Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Asia "Central Asia") intermittently overran India's northern plains.[[42]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Combined-13-54) The resulting [Delhi Sultanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Delhi_Sultanate "Delhi Sultanate") drew northern India into the cosmopolitan [networks of medieval Islam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_Golden_Age "Islamic Golden Age").[[43]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Combined-10-55) In south India, the [Vijayanagara Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vijayanagara_Empire "Vijayanagara Empire") created a long-lasting composite Hindu culture.[[44]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-AsherAsher2006-74-56) In the [Punjab](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Punjab "Punjab"), [Sikhism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sikhism "Sikhism") emerged, rejecting institutionalised religion.[[45]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-AsherAsher2006-267-57) The [Mughal Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mughal_Empire "Mughal Empire") ushered in two centuries of economic expansion and relative peace,[[46]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-AsherAsher2006-152-58) and left [a rich architectural legacy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mughal_architecture "Mughal architecture").[[47]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Fisher2018-106-59)[[48]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAsher1992[httpsarchiveorgdetailsarchitectureofmu0000ashepage250mode2up_250]-60) Gradually expanding [rule of the British East India Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Company_rule_in_India "Company rule in India") turned India into a colonial economy but consolidated its [sovereignty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereignty "Sovereignty").[[49]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Combined-11-61) [British Crown rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Raj "British Raj") began in 1858. The rights promised to Indians were granted slowly,[[50]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Combined-12-62)[[51]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPeers2013[httpsbooksgooglecombooksiddyQuAgAAQBAJpgPA76_76]-63) but [technological changes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Industrial_Revolution "Industrial Revolution") were introduced, and modern ideas of education and the public life took root.[[52]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-EmbreeHay1988-64) [A nationalist movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_independence_movement "Indian independence movement") emerged in India, the first in the non-European [British Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Empire "British Empire") and an influence on other nationalist movements.[[53]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Marshall2001-65)[[54]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Chiriyankandath2016-66) Noted for nonviolent resistance after 1920,[[55]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-metcalf-metcalf-gandhi-1920-67) it became the primary factor in ending British rule.[[56]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-stein-arnold-successful-nationalism-68) In 1947, the British Indian Empire was [partitioned](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Partition_of_India "Partition of India") into two independent [dominions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dominion#India_and_Pakistan "Dominion"), a Hindu-majority [dominion of India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dominion_of_India "Dominion of India") and a Muslim-majority [dominion of Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dominion_of_Pakistan "Dominion of Pakistan"). A large-scale loss of life and an unprecedented migration accompanied the partition.[[57]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-69)

India has been a [federal republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal_republic "Federal republic") since 1950, governed through a democratic [parliamentary system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parliamentary_system "Parliamentary system"). It is a [pluralistic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pluralism_(political_philosophy) "Pluralism (political philosophy)"), [multilingual](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multilingualism "Multilingualism") and [multi-ethnic society](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multiculturalism "Multiculturalism"). India's population grew from 361 million in 1951 to over 1.4 billion in 2023.[[58]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Dyson2018-219-70) During this time, its nominal [per capita income](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_capita_income "Per capita income") increased from US$64 annually to US$2,601, and its literacy rate from 16.6% to 74%.[[59]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Fisher2018-8-71) The [Indian economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy_of_India "Economy of India") has since become a [fast-growing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_real_GDP_growth_rate "List of countries by real GDP growth rate") major economy and [a hub for information technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Information_technology_in_India "Information technology in India"), with an expanding middle class.[[60]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-MetcalfMetcalf2012-265-72) India has reduced [its poverty rate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poverty_in_India "Poverty in India"), though at the cost of increasing economic inequality.[[61]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Dyson2018-216-a-73) It is a [nuclear-weapon state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_states_with_nuclear_weapons "List of states with nuclear weapons") that [ranks high in military expenditure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_military_expenditures "List of countries by military expenditures"). It has disputes over [Kashmir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kashmir "Kashmir") with its neighbours, Pakistan and China, unresolved since the mid-20th century.[[62]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-kashmir-disputes-74) Among the socio-economic challenges India faces are [gender inequality](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gender_inequality_in_India "Gender inequality in India"), [child malnutrition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malnutrition_in_India "Malnutrition in India"),[[63]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-NarayanJohn2018-lead-75) and rising levels of [air pollution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Air_pollution_in_India "Air pollution in India").[[64]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-BalakrishnanDey2019-lead-76) India's land is [megadiverse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Megadiverse_country "Megadiverse country") with four [biodiversity hotspots](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biodiversity_hotspots "Biodiversity hotspots"). [India's wildlife](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wildlife_of_India "Wildlife of India"), which has traditionally been viewed with tolerance in [its culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_of_India "Culture of India"), is supported in [protected habitats](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protected_areas_of_India "Protected areas of India").[[65]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKaranthGopal2005374-77)

Further [etymons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Etymon "Etymon") are: [Hellenistic Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hellenistic_Greek "Hellenistic Greek") *India* (Ἰνδία

## History

Main article: [History of India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_India "History of India")

### Ancient India

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Battle_at_Lanka%2C_Ramayana%2C_Udaipur%2C_1649-53.jpg/250px-Battle_at_Lanka%2C_Ramayana%2C_Udaipur%2C_1649-53.jpg)](File:Battle_at_Lanka,_Ramayana,_Udaipur,_1649-53.jpg)

Manuscript illustration, c. 1650, of the Sanskrit epic [Ramayana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ramayana "Ramayana"), composed in story-telling fashion c. 400 BCE – c. 300 CE[[75]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-87)


[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Cave_26%2C_Ajanta.jpg/250px-Cave_26%2C_Ajanta.jpg)](File:Cave_26,_Ajanta.jpg)

Cave 26, a [Buddhist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buddhism "Buddhism") shrine, of the rock-cut [Ajanta Caves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ajanta_Caves "Ajanta Caves")

Based on [coalescence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coalescent_theory "Coalescent theory") of [Mitochondrial DNA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mitochondrial_DNA "Mitochondrial DNA") and [Y Chromosome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Y_Chromosome "Y Chromosome") data, it is thought that the earliest extant [lineages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lineage_(evolution) "Lineage (evolution)") of anatomically modern humans or *[Homo sapiens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homo_sapiens "Homo sapiens")* on the Indian subcontinent had reached there from Africa between 80,000 and 50,000 years ago, and with high likelihood by 55,000 years ago.[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-PetragliaAllchin-36)[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Dyson2018p1-37)[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Fisher2018p23-38)[[76]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-mattern-slow-moon-non-african-migration-88) Their long occupation, initially in varying forms of isolation as hunter-gatherers, has made the region highly diverse, second only to Africa in human [genetic diversity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genetic_diversity "Genetic diversity").[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Dyson2018-28a-39) However, the earliest known modern human fossils in South Asia date to about 30,000 years ago.[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Dyson2018p1-37) Evidence for the [neolithic period](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neolithic_period "Neolithic period") in the western margins of the [Indus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indus_River "Indus River") [river basin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/River_basin "River basin"), at [Mehrgarh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mehrgarh "Mehrgarh") in [Balochistan, Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balochistan,_Pakistan "Balochistan, Pakistan"), dates to after 7000 BCE. Domestication of grain-producing plants (including [barley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barley "Barley")) and animals (including [humped zebu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zebu "Zebu") cattle) occurred here. These cultures gradually evolved into the [Indus Valley Civilisation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indus_Valley_Civilisation "Indus Valley Civilisation"), which flourished during 2500–1900 BCE in Pakistan and western India.[[77]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWright201045–51-89)[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Combined-2-40) Centred around cities such as [Mohenjo-daro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mohenjo-daro "Mohenjo-daro"), [Harappa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harappa "Harappa"), [Dholavira](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dholavira "Dholavira"), [Ganweriwala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ganweriwala "Ganweriwala"), and [Rakhigarhi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rakhigarhi "Rakhigarhi"),[[78]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWright2010107-90) its characteristic features included standardised weights, [steatite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soapstone "Soapstone") [seals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indus_Valley_Civilisation#Seals "Indus Valley Civilisation"), a written script, urban planning, public works, and arts and crafts including pottery styles, terracotta human figures and animal statuettes.[[78]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWright2010107-90) Networks of towns and villages grew around the cities in a new [agro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agriculture "Agriculture")-[pastoral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Animal_husbandry "Animal husbandry") economy.[[79]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWright2010145–146-91)

Between 1500 BCE and 1200 BCE, an [archaic form](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-language "Proto-language") of [Sanskrit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sanskrit "Sanskrit"), an [Indo-European language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indo-European_language "Indo-European language"), [diffused](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trans-cultural_diffusion "Trans-cultural diffusion") into India from the northwest. Its evidence today is found in the [Rig Veda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rig_Veda "Rig Veda")—the oldest scripture associated with what later became [Hinduism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hinduism "Hinduism")—which was composed by [Indo-Aryan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indo-Aryan_languages "Indo-Aryan languages")-speaking tribes migrating east from what is today northern [Afghanistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Afghanistan "Afghanistan") and across the [Punjab region](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Punjab_region "Punjab region").[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-lowe1-41)[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Combined-4-Rigveda-42) The settling of the [Ganges](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ganges_river "Ganges river") [river plain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/River_plain "River plain") took place during the next millennium, when large swathes of the river system's adjoining regions were deforested, at times by setting fires, or later by employing iron implements, and prepared for agriculture. The settlement may have involved driving the preexisting people out or enslaving them.[[80]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDyson201816,_25-92) The [Dravidian languages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dravidian_languages "Dravidian languages") of India were supplanted in the north, creating a broad [language family](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Language_family "Language family")-divide, with the Indo-Aryan languages being spoken mainly in the [north](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_India "North India") and [west](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_India "West India"), and the Dravidian in some parts of [east India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_India "East India") and most of the [south](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_India "South India").[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Combined-4-44) [Classical Sanskrit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classical_Sanskrit "Classical Sanskrit"), a refined and standardised grammatical form would emerge in the mid-1st millennium BCE and was codified in the *[Aṣṭādhyāyī](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aṣṭādhyāyī "Aṣṭādhyāyī")* ('Eight chapters') of [Pāṇini](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pāṇini "Pāṇini").[[m]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-94) The two major Sanskrit epics, the [Mahābhārata](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahābhārata "Mahābhārata") and the [Rāmāyaṇa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rāmāyaṇa "Rāmāyaṇa"), however, were composed in a range of oral storytelling registers called [Epic Sanskrit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epic_Sanskrit "Epic Sanskrit") which was used in northern India between 400 BCE and 300 CE, and roughly contemporary with classical Sanskrit.[[82]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Lowe2017-epic-95)

A second [urbanisation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urbanisation "Urbanisation") had taken place in [South Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asia "South Asia") by 400 BCE, this time on the Ganges plain. In fortified cities, [social differentiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_differentiation "Social differentiation") by [caste](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caste "Caste"), or [varna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Varna_(Hinduism) "Varna (Hinduism)"), had emerged.[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Dyson2018-16a-45) By the mid-millennium two new ethical and social systems had arisen: [Jainism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_Jainism "History of Jainism") based on the teachings of [Mahavira](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahavira "Mahavira") and [Buddhism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_Buddhism_in_India "History of Buddhism in India") on those of [the Buddha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Buddha "The Buddha"). Both religions stressed non-violence and abjured animal sacrifices conducted in [Brahmanism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Historical_Vedic_religion#Brahmanism "Historical Vedic religion"),[[n]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-96) and birth into a fixed hereditary *varna*. By living ethically, lay people could rise socially and morally in these religions.[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-Fisher2018-59-46) Chronicling the life of the Buddha was central to the beginnings of recorded history in India.[[83]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStein201021,_61–62-97) The rise of the two religions was a backdrop to the emergence of the first loose-knit geographically extensive power in South Asia, the [Maurya Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maurya_Empire "Maurya Empire"). During the rule of the founder's grandson, [Ashoka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ashoka "Ashoka") (ca. 268–232 BCE), the empire briefly controlled the major urban hubs and arteries of the subcontinent, except in the deep south.[[84]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDyson201816–17-98)[[o]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-100)[[p]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-102) The empire's period was notable for creativity in art, architecture, inscriptions, and produced texts,[[87]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTELudden201428–29-103) but also for the declining rights of women in the mainstream [Indo-Aryan speaking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indo-Aryan_languages "Indo-Aryan languages") regions.[[88]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDyson201819-104) Following his [conquest of Kalinga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kalinga_War "Kalinga War"), in which his forces caused immense loss of life, Ashoka adopted [Buddhism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buddhism "Buddhism"), subsequently adopting a policy of *dhamma* and commissioning numerous [rock and pillar edicts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edicts_of_Ashoka "Edicts of Ashoka") throughout the Maurya Empire to promote ethical conduct, non-violence, and social welfare.[[89]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStein201073-105) As the edicts forbade both the killing of wild animals and the destruction of forests, Ashoka is seen by some modern environmental historians as an early embodiment of that ethos.[[90]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-elverskog-2020-lead-1-106)[[91]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFisher201872-107)

By the 4th and 5th centuries, the [Gupta Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gupta_Empire "Gupta Empire") had created a complex system of administration and taxation in the greater Ganges Plain;[[92]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKulkeRothermund2016118,_126-108) this system became a model for later Indian kingdoms.[[93]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTERobb201168–69-109) Under the Guptas, a renewed Hinduism based on devotion, rather than the management of ritual, began to assert itself.[[94]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStein201093–94-110) The renewal was reflected in a flowering of art, literature, and science.[[95]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGilbert201754–55-111)[[39]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKulkeRothermund2016122–123-50) In South India, the [Sangam literature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sangam_literature "Sangam literature") of the [Tamil language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tamil_language "Tamil language") reveals that, between 200 BCE and 200 CE, the southern peninsula was ruled by the [Cheras](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chera_dynasty "Chera dynasty") and the [Cholas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chola_dynasty "Chola dynasty"), along the western and eastern plains, respectively, of the [Kaveri river](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kaveri_river "Kaveri river") valley, and the [Pandyas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pandya "Pandya") farther south along the [Vaigai river](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaigai_river "Vaigai river") valley.[[96]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStein201098-112) By the sixth century, the [Pallavas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pallava "Pallava") had grown into a regional power. Simultaneously, Buddhism and Jainism, which had favoured a conservative [transactionalism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transactionalism "Transactionalism"), were replaced by kingly devotion to the gods of particular places, which became a characteristic of the [Bhakti movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bhakti_movement "Bhakti movement").[[97]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStein2010100–101-113) The Pallavas, in particular, [traded extensively with the Roman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indo-Roman_trade_relations "Indo-Roman trade relations") and with [West](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Asia "Western Asia") and [Southeast Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southeast_Asia "Southeast Asia").[[98]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStein201099-114)

Calligraphy on the [Qutb Minar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qutb_Minar "Qutb Minar"), built in the [Delhi sultanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Delhi_sultanate "Delhi sultanate") from 1199 CE to 1220 CE


The Indian early medieval age, from 600 to 1200 CE, was defined by regional kingdoms and cultural diversity.[[99]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStein2010116-115) No ruler of this period was able to create an empire and consistently control lands much beyond their core region.[[q]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-117)[[100]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStein2010115–116-116) During this time, pastoral peoples, whose land had been cleared to make way for the growing agricultural economy, were accommodated within caste society, as were new non-traditional ruling classes.[[101]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStein2010116–117-118) The caste system consequently began to show regional differences.[[101]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStein2010116–117-118)

In the 6th and 7th centuries, the first [devotional hymns](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bhakti "Bhakti") were composed in Tamil.[[102]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStein2010118-119) They were imitated all over India and led to both the resurgence of Hinduism and the development of all [modern languages of the subcontinent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Languages_of_India "Languages of India").[[102]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStein2010118-119) Indian royalty, big and small, and the temples they patronised drew citizens in significant numbers to the capital cities, which became economic hubs as well.[[103]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStein2010118–119-120) Temple towns of various sizes began to appear everywhere as India underwent another urbanisation.[[103]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStein2010118–119-120) By the 8th and 9th centuries, the effects were felt in Southeast Asia, as South Indian culture and political systems were exported to lands that became part of modern-day [Myanmar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myanmar "Myanmar"), [Thailand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thailand "Thailand"), [Laos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laos "Laos"), [Brunei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brunei "Brunei"), [Cambodia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cambodia "Cambodia"), [Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnam "Vietnam"), [Philippines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philippines "Philippines"), [Malaysia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaysia "Malaysia"), and [Indonesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indonesia "Indonesia").[[104]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStein2010121–123-121) Indian merchants, scholars, and sometimes armies were involved in this transmission; Southeast Asians took the initiative as well, with many sojourning in Indian seminaries and translating Buddhist and Hindu texts into their languages.[[104]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStein2010121–123-121)

After the 10th century, Muslim Central Asian nomadic clans, using [swift-horse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Courser_(horse) "Courser (horse)") cavalry and raising vast armies united by ethnicity, repeatedly overran South Asia's north-western plains,[[r]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-123) leading eventually to the establishment of the Islamic [Delhi Sultanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Delhi_Sultanate "Delhi Sultanate") in 1206.[[106]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTELudden201485-124) The sultanate was to control much of North India and to make many forays into South India. Although at first disruptive for the Indian elites, the sultanate largely left its vast non-Muslim subject population to its own laws and customs.[[107]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAsherTalbot202254-125)[[108]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf201241-126)

By repeatedly repulsing [Mongol raiders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongol_Empire "Mongol Empire") in the 13th century, the sultanate saved India from the devastation visited on West and Central Asia,[[106]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTELudden201485-124) setting the scene for centuries of [migration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_migration "Human migration") of fleeing soldiers, learned men, mystics, traders, artists, and artisans from that region into the subcontinent, thereby creating a syncretic Indo-Islamic culture in the north.[[109]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAsherTalbot200850–51-127) The sultanate's raiding and weakening of the regional kingdoms of South India paved the way for the indigenous [Vijayanagara Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vijayanagara_Empire "Vijayanagara Empire").[[110]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAsherTalbot200853-128) Embracing a strong [Shaivite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shaivism "Shaivism") tradition and building upon the military technology of the sultanate, the empire came to control a large portion of peninsular India,[[111]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf201247-129) and was to influence South Indian society for long afterwards.[[110]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAsherTalbot200853-128)

### Early modern India

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Shaikh_Salim_Chishti_Ka_Maqbara.jpg/250px-Shaikh_Salim_Chishti_Ka_Maqbara.jpg)](File:Shaikh_Salim_Chishti_Ka_Maqbara.jpg)

The *[dargah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dargah "Dargah")* of [Sufi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sufi "Sufi") [Salim Chisti](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salim_Chisti "Salim Chisti"), built by [Mughal emperor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mughal_Empire "Mughal Empire"), [Akbar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akbar "Akbar"), early 17th century

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/India_1835_2_Mohurs.jpg/250px-India_1835_2_Mohurs.jpg)](File:India_1835_2_Mohurs.jpg)

An [East India Company rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Company_rule_in_India "Company rule in India") gold [mohur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mohur "Mohur"), issued during the reign of [William IIII](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_IV "William IV")

In the early 16th century, northern India, then under mainly Muslim rulers,[[112]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTERobb201197&ndash;98-130) fell again to the superior mobility and firepower of a new generation of Central Asian warriors.[[113]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStein2010159-131) The resulting [Mughal Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mughal_Empire "Mughal Empire") did not stamp out the local societies it came to rule. Instead, it balanced and pacified them through new administrative practices[[114]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAsherTalbot2022136-132)[[115]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTERobb2011108–109-133) and diverse and inclusive ruling elites,[[116]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf201215-134) leading to more systematic, centralised, and uniform rule.[[117]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAsherTalbot2022136,_151–152-135) Eschewing tribal bonds and Islamic identity, especially under [Akbar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akbar "Akbar"), the Mughals united their far-flung realms through loyalty—expressed through a Persianised culture—to an emperor who had near-divine status.[[118]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf201217-136)

The Mughal state's economic policies, deriving most revenues from agriculture[[119]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAsherTalbot2008158-137) and mandating that taxes be paid in the well-regulated silver currency,[[120]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStein2010164-138) caused peasants and artisans to enter larger markets.[[121]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAsherTalbot2022187-139) The relative peace maintained by the empire during much of the 17th century was a factor in India's economic expansion,[[121]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAsherTalbot2022187-139) resulting in greater patronage of [painting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mughal_painting "Mughal painting"), literary forms, textiles, and [architecture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mughal_architecture "Mughal architecture").[[122]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAsherTalbot2008186-140) Newly coherent social groups in northern and western India, such as the [Marathas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maratha_Empire "Maratha Empire"), the [Rajputs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rajput "Rajput"), and the [Sikhs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sikh_Empire "Sikh Empire"), gained military and governing ambitions during Mughal rule, which, through collaboration or adversity, gave them both recognition and military experience.[[123]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf200623–24-141) Expanding commerce during Mughal rule gave rise to new Indian commercial and political elites along the coasts of southern and eastern India.[[123]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf200623–24-141) The incremental fragmentation of imperial authority during the late 17th and 18th centuries facilitated a process of decentralisation, enabling provincial elites to assert localised autonomy and consolidate control over their own affairs.[[124]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAsherTalbot2008256-142)

By the early 18th century, with the lines between commercial enterprise and political sovereignty increasingly blurred, European chartered companies—notably the [English East India Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_India_Company "East India Company")—solidified their presence through fortified coastal outposts.[[125]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAsherTalbot2008286-143)[[126]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf200644–49-144) The East India Company's control of the seas, greater resources, and more advanced military training and technology led it to assert its military strength increasingly and caused it to become attractive to a portion of the Indian elite; these factors were crucial in allowing the company to gain control over the [Bengal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bengal "Bengal") region by 1765 and sideline the other European companies.[[127]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTERobb2011115–117-145)[[125]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAsherTalbot2008286-143)[[128]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTELudden2002128–132-146)[[129]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf200651–55-147) Its further access to the riches of Bengal and the subsequent increased strength and size of its army enabled it to annexe or subdue most of India by the 1820s.[[130]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf200668–71-148) India was no longer exporting manufactured goods as it long had, but instead supplying the [British Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Empire "British Empire") with raw materials. Many historians consider this to be the onset of India's colonial period.[[125]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAsherTalbot2008286-143) By this time, with its economic power severely curtailed by the British parliament and having effectively been made an arm of British administration, the East India Company began more consciously to enter non-economic arenas, including education, social reform, and culture.[[131]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAsherTalbot2008289-149)

### Modern India

Main article: [History of India (1947–present)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_India_(1947–present) "History of India (1947–present)")

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/Mahatma_Gandhi_leaves_Presidency_Jail_in_Calcutta.jpg/250px-Mahatma_Gandhi_leaves_Presidency_Jail_in_Calcutta.jpg)](File:Mahatma_Gandhi_leaves_Presidency_Jail_in_Calcutta.jpg)

Mahatma Gandhi after a visit to the Presidency Jail, [Calcutta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calcutta "Calcutta"), April 1938

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Palace_of_Assembly_Chandigarh_2006.jpg/250px-Palace_of_Assembly_Chandigarh_2006.jpg)](File:Palace_of_Assembly_Chandigarh_2006.jpg)

The [Capitol Complex](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chandigarh_Capitol_Complex "Chandigarh Capitol Complex") in [Chandigarh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chandigarh "Chandigarh"), commissioned after India's [1947 partition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Partition_of_India "Partition of India")

The appointment in 1848 of [Lord Dalhousie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Broun-Ramsay,_1st_Marquess_of_Dalhousie "James Broun-Ramsay, 1st Marquess of Dalhousie") as Governor General of the East India Company set the stage for changes essential to a modern state: the consolidation and demarcation of sovereignty, the surveillance of the population, and the education of citizens. Technological changes—among them, railways, canals, and the telegraph—were introduced not long after their introduction in [Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europe "Europe").[[132]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTERobb2011169–171-150)[[133]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf200694–99-151)[[134]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBrown199483-152)[[135]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPeers200650-153) Disaffection with the company also grew during this time and set off the [Indian Rebellion of 1857](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Rebellion_of_1857 "Indian Rebellion of 1857"). Fed by diverse resentments and perceptions, including invasive British-style social reforms, harsh land taxes, and summary treatment of some wealthy landowners and princes, the rebellion rocked many regions of northern and central India and shook the foundations of Company rule.[[136]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf2006100–103-154)[[137]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBrown199485–86-155) After the rebellion was suppressed in 1858, the East India Company was disbanded, and the British government began to [directly administer India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Raj "British Raj"). Proclaiming a [unitary state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unitary_state "Unitary state") and a gradual but limited British-style parliamentary system,[[138]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStein2010227-156) the new rulers also protected princes and landed gentry as a feudal safeguard against future unrest.[[139]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf2006103–108-157) In the decades following, public life gradually emerged across India, eventually leading to the founding of the [Indian National Congress](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_National_Congress "Indian National Congress") in 1885.[[140]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTERobb2001183-158)[[141]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTESarkar19831–4-159)[[142]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTECopland2001ix–x-160)[[143]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf2006123-161)

The rush of technology and the commercialisation of agriculture in the second half of the 19th century was marked by economic setbacks, and many small farmers became dependent on the whims of faraway markets.[[144]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStein2010249-162) There was an increase in the number of large-scale [famines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Famine_in_India "Famine in India"),[[145]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStein2010245-163) and, despite the risks of infrastructure development borne by Indian taxpayers, little industrial employment was generated for Indians.[[146]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStein2010247–248-164) However, commercial cropping, especially in the newly canalled Punjab, led to increased food production for internal consumption.[[147]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf2006126-165) The railway network provided critical famine relief,[[148]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf200697-166) notably reduced the cost of moving goods,[[148]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf200697-166) and helped nascent Indian-owned industry.[[147]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf2006126-165)

After [World War I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_I "World War I"), in which more than [1.3 million Indians served](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Army_during_World_War_I "Indian Army during World War I"),[[149]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf2006163-167) a new period began. It was marked by [British reforms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Montagu–Chelmsford_Reforms "Montagu–Chelmsford Reforms") but also [repressive legislation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rowlatt_act "Rowlatt act"), by more strident Indian calls for self-rule, and by the beginnings of a [nonviolent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonviolent "Nonviolent") movement of non-co-operation led by [Mahatma Gandhi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahatma_Gandhi "Mahatma Gandhi").[[150]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf2006167-168) During the 1930s, the British enacted slow legislative reform; the Indian National Congress won victories in the resulting elections.[[151]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf2006195–197-169) The next decade was beset with crises: [Indian participation in World War II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India_in_World_War_II "India in World War II"), the Congress's final push for non-co-operation, and an upsurge of [Muslim nationalism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muslim_nationalism_in_South_Asia "Muslim nationalism in South Asia"). All were capped by the advent of independence in 1947, but tempered by the [partition of India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Partition_of_India "Partition of India") into two states: India and Pakistan.[[152]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf2006203-170)

India's constitution was adopted in 1950 and established a secular, democratic republic.[[153]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf2006231-171) Economic liberalisation has created a large urban middle class and transformed India into a [fast growing economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_(real)_growth_rate "List of countries by GDP (real) growth rate").[[154]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-exp-middle-class-172)[[60]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-MetcalfMetcalf2012-265-72) However, India has been hamstrung by persistent poverty, both rural and urban;[[155]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf2006265–266-173) [religious](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Religious_violence_in_India "Religious violence in India")- and [caste-related violence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caste-related_violence_in_India "Caste-related violence in India");[[156]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf2006266–270-174) [Maoist-inspired Naxalite insurgencies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naxalite–Maoist_insurgency "Naxalite–Maoist insurgency");[[157]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf2006253-175) and [separatism in Jammu and Kashmir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Insurgency_in_Jammu_and_Kashmir "Insurgency in Jammu and Kashmir") and [in Northeast India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Insurgency_in_Northeast_India "Insurgency in Northeast India").[[158]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf2006274-176) India has unresolved territorial disputes with [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China–India_relations#1960s "China–India relations") and with [Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indo-Pakistani_wars_and_conflicts "Indo-Pakistani wars and conflicts").[[159]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMetcalfMetcalf2006247–248-177)

## Geography

See also: [Geography of India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geography_of_India "Geography of India")

India accounts for the bulk of the Indian subcontinent, lying atop the [Indian tectonic plate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Plate "Indian Plate"), and a part of the [Indo-Australian Plate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indo-Australian_Plate "Indo-Australian Plate").[[160]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAliAitchison2005-178) India's defining geologic processes began approximately 70 million years ago, when the Indian Plate, then part of the southern supercontinent [Gondwana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gondwana "Gondwana"), began a north-eastward [drift](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plate_tectonics "Plate tectonics") caused by [seafloor spreading](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seafloor_spreading "Seafloor spreading") to its south-west, and later, south and south-east.[[160]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAliAitchison2005-178) Simultaneously, the vast [Tethyan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tethys_Ocean "Tethys Ocean") [oceanic crust](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oceanic_crust "Oceanic crust"), to its northeast, began to [subduct](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subduction "Subduction") under the [Eurasian Plate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eurasian_Plate "Eurasian Plate").[[160]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAliAitchison2005-178) The [Indian continental crust](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_plate "Indian plate"), however, was obstructed and was sheared horizontally. Its lower crust and mantle slid under, but the upper layer piled up in sheets (or [nappes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nappe "Nappe")) ahead of the subduction zone.[[161]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMolnar2015117-179) This created the [orogeny](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orogeny "Orogeny"), or process of mountain building, of the Himalayas.[[162]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMolnar2015118-180) The middle and stiffer layer continued to push into Tibet, causing [crustal thickening](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thrust_tectonics "Thrust tectonics") of the [Tibetan Plateau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tibetan_Plateau "Tibetan Plateau").[[163]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMolnar2015128-181) Immediately south of the emerging Himalayas, plate movement created a vast crescent-shaped [trough](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trough_(geology) "Trough (geology)") that rapidly filled with river-borne sediment[[164]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDikshitSchwartzberg20237-182) and now constitutes the [Indo-Gangetic Plain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indo-Gangetic_Plain "Indo-Gangetic Plain").[[165]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPrakash_et_al.2000-183) The original Indian plate makes its first appearance above the sediment in the ancient [Aravalli range](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aravalli_range "Aravalli range"), which extends from the [Delhi Ridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Delhi_Ridge "Delhi Ridge") in a southwesterly direction. To the west lies the [Thar Desert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thar_Desert "Thar Desert"), the eastern spread of which is checked by the Aravallis.[[166]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-aravalli-184)[[167]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-prasad-aravalli-185)[[168]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-fisher-aravalli-186)

The remaining Indian Plate survives as [peninsular India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_India "South India"), the oldest and geologically most stable part of India. It extends as far north as the [Satpura](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Satpura_Range "Satpura Range") and [Vindhya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vindhya_Range "Vindhya Range") ranges in central India. These parallel chains run from the Arabian Sea coast in Gujarat in the west to the coal-rich [Chota Nagpur Plateau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chota_Nagpur_Plateau "Chota Nagpur Plateau") in Jharkhand in the east.[[169]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDikshitSchwartzberg20238-187) To the south, the remaining peninsular landmass, the [Deccan Plateau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deccan_Plateau "Deccan Plateau"), is flanked on the west and east by coastal ranges known as the [Western](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Ghats "Western Ghats") and [Eastern Ghats](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Ghats "Eastern Ghats");[[170]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDikshitSchwartzberg20239–10-188) the plateau contains the country's oldest rock formations, some over one billion years old. Constituted in such fashion, India lies to the north of the equator between 6° 44′ and 35° 30′ north latitude[[s]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-189) and 68° 7′ and 97° 25′ east longitude.[[171]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMinistry_of_Information_and_Broadcasting20071-190)

Major Himalayan-origin rivers that substantially flow through India include the [Ganges](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ganges "Ganges") and the [Brahmaputra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brahmaputra_River "Brahmaputra River"), both of which drain into the [Bay of Bengal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal "Bay of Bengal").[[172]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDikshitSchwartzberg202315-191) Important tributaries of the Ganges include the [Yamuna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yamuna "Yamuna") and the [Kosi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kosi_River "Kosi River"). The Kosi's extremely low gradient, caused by long-term silt deposition, leads to severe floods and course changes.[[173]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDuff1993353-192)[[174]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBasuXavier2017[httpsbooksgooglecombooksidnXmLDgAAQBAJpgPA78_78]-193) Major peninsular rivers, whose steeper gradients prevent their waters from flooding, include the [Godavari](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Godavari_River "Godavari River"), the [Mahanadi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahanadi_River "Mahanadi River"), the [Kaveri](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kaveri_River "Kaveri River"), and the [Krishna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Krishna_River "Krishna River"), which also drain into the Bay of Bengal;[[175]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDikshitSchwartzberg202316-194) and the [Narmada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Narmada_River "Narmada River") and the [Tapti](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tapti_River "Tapti River"), which drain into the [Arabian Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabian_Sea "Arabian Sea").[[176]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDikshitSchwartzberg202317-195)

India's coastline measures 7,517 kilometres (4,700 mi) in length; of this distance, 5,423 kilometres (3,400 mi) belong to peninsular India and 2,094 kilometres (1,300 mi) to the Andaman, Nicobar, and Lakshadweep island chains.[[177]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKumarPathakPednekarRaju2006-196) According to the Indian naval hydrographic charts, the [mainland coastline](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coastal_India "Coastal India") consists of the following: 43% sandy beaches; 11% rocky shores, including cliffs; and 46% [mudflats](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mudflat "Mudflat") or marshy shores.[[177]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKumarPathakPednekarRaju2006-196) Coastal features include the marshy [Rann of Kutch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rann_of_Kutch "Rann of Kutch") of western India and the alluvial [Sundarbans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sundarbans "Sundarbans") delta of eastern India; the latter is shared with Bangladesh.[[178]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDikshitSchwartzberg202312-197) India has two [archipelagos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archipelago "Archipelago"): the [Lakshadweep](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lakshadweep "Lakshadweep") [coral atolls](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atoll "Atoll") off India's south-western coast; and the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, a volcanic chain in the [Andaman Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andaman_Sea "Andaman Sea").[[179]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDikshitSchwartzberg202313-198)

India has [28 states and 8 union territories](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Indian_states_and_union_territories_by_Human_Development_Index "List of Indian states and union territories by Human Development Index").
